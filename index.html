Who I Am

I’ve always been drawn to communication - to the way ideas move between people, and how the same message can land differently depending on context.

I began my career in PR and strategic communications, working in environments where clarity, positioning and timing truly mattered.

Over time, I became curious not only about what we say, but about how people actually process it - what captures attention, what builds perception, and what makes something stick.

That curiosity led me to study neuroscience and psychology alongside my professional path.

Today, I work in technology marketing, translating complex systems - hardware, software and AI - into structured narratives and visual content that make sense to real people.

I enjoy shaping stories from the strategic foundation all the way to execution, and seeing how thoughtful communication can genuinely connect.
